Indian Music Genres

Classical

Rooted in ancient traditions, Classical music forms the foundation of Indian musical culture. Indian classical music is the classical music of the Indian subcontinent. It is generally described using terms like Shastriya Sangeet and Marg Sangeet. It has two major traditions: the North Indian classical music known as Hindustani and the South Indian expression known as Carnatic. These traditions were not distinct until about the 15th century.

Bollywood

Popular music from India's film industry, blending various genres with catchy beats. Bollywood film songs have been described as eclectic both in instrumentation and style. They often employ foreign instruments and rework existing songs, showing remarkable inventiveness in the reinvention of melodies and instrumental techniques.

Folk & Culture

Music that expresses the heart of Indian villages and regional cultures. Indian folk music is diverse because of India's enormous cultural and traditional diversity. It is sung in various languages and dialects throughout the length and breadth of this vast nation and exported to different parts of the world owing to migration.

    Lata Mangeshkar

    Lata Mangeshkar (1929–2022) was an iconic Indian playback singer, known as the "Nightingale of India." She sang in over 36 languages and recorded thousands of
    songs spanning seven decades.
    Her melodious voice, incredible range, and emotive delivery made her a legendary figure in Indian music.
    Some of her timeless hits include "Lag Ja Gale," "Pyar Kiya To Darna Kya," and "Ae Mere Watan Ke Logon."
    She received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna (2001) and Padma Bhushan (1969). Despite her fame, she led a private life and never married.
    Lata Mangeshkar passed away on February 6, 2022, leaving behind an unparalleled musical legacy.

    Nusrat Fathe Ali Khan

    **Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan** (1948–1997) was a legendary Pakistani qawwali singer known for his powerful voice and mastery of Sufi music.
    He brought qawwali to international audiences, blending traditional sounds with contemporary styles. His iconic songs include *"Afreen Afreen"* and *"Tere Bin Nahin Jeena."*
    Nusrat collaborated with artists like **Peter Gabriel** and performed globally, earning numerous awards, including Pakistan’s **Pride of Performance Award**.
    His influence on both traditional and world music continues to inspire artists today.
